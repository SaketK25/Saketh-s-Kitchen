Ingredients:

  • 2 Cups Basmati rice
  • 4-5 Cups Water,1 bay leaf,4-5 cloves
  • 2 green cardamoms,1-inch cinnamon stick
  • 1 tsp salt

For vegetables:

  • 2 cups (carrots, beans, peas, potatoes, etc.)
  • 1 onion, 2 tomatoes, 1 tsp ginger-garlic paste, 2 tbsp curd, mint, and coriander leaves

For Spices:

  • 2 tsp biryani masala, 1 tsp turmeric, 1 tsp red chili powder, 1 tsp garam masala

Instructions:

  1. Cook the Rice:
    • Wash and soak basmati rice for 30 minutes.
    • In a large pot, boil water with salt and whole spices (bay leaf, cloves, cardamom, cinnamon).
    • Add soaked rice and cook until 70% done. Drain and set aside.
  2. Prepare the Vegetables:
    • Heat oil or ghee in a pan. SautÃ© cashew nuts and raisins (if using) until golden and set aside. Add sliced onions and fry until golden brown. Add ginger-garlic paste and sautÃ© until raw smell disappears. Add tomatoes and cook until soft. Add diced vegetables, biryani masala, turmeric, chili powder, coriander powder, and salt. Mix well. Add 1 tbsp curd and cook covered until vegetables are tender.
  3. Layer the Biryani:
    • In a heavy-bottomed pot or pressure cooker, add a layer of cooked vegetables. Add a layer of rice. Sprinkle some mint leaves, coriander leaves, and saffron milk (if using). Repeat the layering until all rice and vegetables are used, finishing with a rice layer.
  4. Dum Cooking
    • Cover the pot tightly with a lid. You can seal it with dough to lock the steam. Cook on low heat for 20-25 minutes or until flavors are well combined.
  5. Serve
    • Gently fluff the biryani with a fork. Garnish with fried cashew nuts, raisins, and coriander leaves. Serve hot with raita or your choice of curry.
